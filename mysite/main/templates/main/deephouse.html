Deep House

Description:

Deep house is a genre of electronic dance music (EDM) that emerged in the 1980s and gained popularity in the 1990s. It is characterized by its smooth, mellow, and atmospheric sound, incorporating elements of soul, jazz, funk, and Chicago house.

Deep house typically features a slower tempo compared to other forms of EDM, with a range of around 110 to 125 beats per minute (BPM). The tracks often have a deep bassline, which gives the genre its name, along with rhythmic percussion, melodic synthesizers, and soulful vocals.
